Daily guidelines
Get outside within 30 minutes of waking
Walk to the nearest open view — beach, waterfront, anywhere with sky. 15–20 minutes. No phone.
Morning light resets your circadian rhythm. This is the single highest-leverage thing in the plan.
Coffee outside, in the sun
First coffee after the walk, not before. Sit outside with it. Face the light. No scrolling while you drink it.
Delays caffeine past the cortisol spike. The sun exposure compounds the circadian benefit.
No phone for the first 30–45 minutes
Leave it in the apartment or in your pocket. The morning belongs to you before it belongs to anyone else.
Three proper meals at roughly the same time each day
Breakfast by 10. Lunch around 1. Dinner by 7. Regular meal timing stabilises energy and reinforces daily structure. You know how to cook — use it.
Protein at every meal
Protein matters when rebuilding muscle from a deconditioned state. Keep the skyr, the salmon/tempeh, the protein at dinner. Add a shake on gym days if needed.
Hydrate before you're thirsty
Lanzarote is warmer and drier than Margate. Carry water on any walk or hike. Dehydration mimics fatigue — you don't need more of that.
Caffeine cut-off: 2pm
Two filter coffees, both before 2pm. Anything that protects sleep is high priority.
RPE 5–6 on everything. If it feels too light, it's correct.
You will want to go heavier. The purpose of these two weeks is reintroduction, not progression. Muscle memory is there — it will come back fast. Rushing it is how you burn out and stop.
Showing up is the session
On a low energy day, the 20-minute low-battery session is the plan. Walking to the gym, touching some weight, and leaving is a win.
Use the conversation test for intensity
If you can't hold a conversation, you're going too hard. Perceived effort is your guide. If it feels easy, it's correct for this phase.
Alternate A and B sessions, add C when you want to
Push/squat one day, hinge/pull next, carry/core if you feel like a third. No more than 3 gym sessions per week. Days between are for walking, swimming, yoga, or nothing.
Start winding down at sunset, not at 8pm in bed
Sunset walk, pool swim, stretch + breathwork, reading outside, visual journal. Shorten the gap between wind-down and sleep.
Extended exhale breathing as the last thing before sleep
Inhale 4 counts, exhale 6–8 counts. 10 minutes lying on your back. Go straight to bed after.
Phone out of reach from 30 minutes before sleep
Not on silent next to you. In another room, or across the room face down.
Target: asleep by 10:30
Not rigid — a direction. If wind-down starts at sunset (~7:30) and you do a walk then breathwork, you'll be naturally ready.
This is diagnostic, not a boot camp
Pay attention to what happens when the stress drops away. Does your energy lift? Does your sleep improve? The answers shape everything that comes next.
On the worst days, the coffee walk and the pool are a complete day
Morning light + one meal cooked properly + getting in water = a legitimate day. Rest is not failure.
Notice, don't track
No step counts, calorie targets, or training logs. Just notice: how do I feel today compared to yesterday? The sketchbook is the only recording tool you need.
The anchors survive disruption
When someone visits, keep the morning walk. Do active things together. Flex everything else. The two bookends hold the structure even when the middle falls apart.
